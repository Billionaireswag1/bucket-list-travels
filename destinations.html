Our Top Destinations for 2026

From cultural capitals to adventure hubs, these hand-picked locations offer the perfect blend of experiences for your bucket list journey.

Kyoto, Japan — Timeless Culture and Cherry Blossoms

Why visit in 2026: Kyoto remains Japan's cultural heart. New immersive tea ceremonies, restored geisha districts, and UNESCO World Heritage sites make this the perfect year to go.

  • Cultural: Fushimi Inari Shrine, Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, Kinkaku-ji Temple
  • Adventure: Day hike to Kurama Temple, cycling through rural villages
  • Events: Aoi Matsuri Festival (May), Jidai Matsuri (October), Cherry Blossom season (March-April)

Best time to visit: March to May for cherry blossoms, or October to November for autumn foliage.

Bucket List Tip: Book a traditional ryokan with an onsen for an authentic Japanese stay.

Milan, Italy — Fashion and Design Capital

Why visit in 2026: Milan's design scene is buzzing with Milan Design Week and new art exhibitions. The city blends historic architecture with cutting-edge fashion and culture.

  • Cultural: Milan's Duomo, Da Vinci's The Last Supper, La Scala Opera House
  • Food: Risotto alla Milanese, Panettone, authentic espresso bars
  • Events: Milan Design Week (April), Milan Fashion Week (February and September)

Best time to visit: April to June for pleasant weather and design events, or September to October for fashion season.

Bucket List Tip: Book rooftop tickets to Duomo in advance for breathtaking city views.

The Dolomites, Italy — Alpine Grandeur

Why visit in 2026: Via ferrata routes, luxury mountain lodges, and summer skiing await. The Dolomites offer some of the most dramatic alpine scenery in the world.

  • Adventure: Hiking Tre Cima, via ferrata climbing, e-biking through Seceda
  • Relaxation: Mountain spas, rifugio stays with panoramic views
  • Events: Dolomiti Balloon Festival (September), Sellaronda Ski Marathon (winter)

Best time to visit: June to September for hiking, December to March for skiing.

Bucket List Tip: Combine a stylish Milan city break with a luxury rifugio stay in the mountains.

Queenstown, New Zealand — Adventure Capital of the World

Why visit in 2026: New eco-lodges and heli-skiing experiences launch this year. Bungy jumping, jet boating, and Milford Sound cruises await adventure seekers.

  • Adventure: Skydiving, Shotover River jet boats, ziplining through forests, bungy jumping
  • Relaxation: Wine tasting in Gibbston Valley, Onsen hot pools with mountain views
  • Events: Queenstown Winter Festival (June), International Marathon (November)

Best time to visit: December to February for summer activities, June to August for skiing.

Bucket List Tip: Extend your trip to Fiordland National Park for world-class hiking and Milford Sound cruises.

Oaxaca, Mexico — Vibrant Culture and Culinary Soul

Why visit in 2026: Oaxaca is a rising star for authentic cultural immersion. Known for mole, mezcal, and ancient Zapotec ruins. New artisan workshops launch this year.

  • Cultural: Monte Albán ruins, Hierve el Agua petrified waterfalls, textile markets
  • Culinary: Cooking classes with local chefs, tlayudas, mezcal tastings, mole negro
  • Events: Day of the Dead (November 1-2), Guelaguetza dance festival (July)

Best time to visit: October to November for Day of the Dead, or December to April for dry weather.

Bucket List Tip: Visit the Sierra Norte for ecotourism and stunning mountain villages.

Mallorca, Spain — Mediterranean Beauty and Hidden Coves

Why visit in 2026: Beyond beach resorts, Mallorca offers Tramuntana mountain hikes, cycling routes, and secluded calas. New sustainable tourism initiatives elevate the island.

  • Adventure: Climbing at Sa Calobra, sea kayaking, caving expeditions, cycling
  • Relaxation: Hidden coves like Caló des Moro, beachfront yoga retreats, spa resorts
  • Events: Pollença Music Festival (August), Mallorca 312 cycling challenge (April)

Best time to visit: May to June or September to October for pleasant weather and fewer crowds.

Bucket List Tip: Explore the village of Deià and enjoy sunset at a seaside chiringuito.

Which Destination Matches Your Travel Style?

  • Adventure Seekers: Queenstown (New Zealand), The Dolomites (Italy), Mallorca (Spain)
  • Culture and Heritage Lovers: Kyoto (Japan), Oaxaca (Mexico), Milan (Italy)
  • Relaxation and Coastal Vibes: Mallorca (Spain), Queenstown (New Zealand)
  • Event-Driven Travelers: Milan Design Week, Day of the Dead in Oaxaca, Cherry Blossom season in Kyoto

Planning Tips for 2026

  • Book early: Kyoto's cherry blossom season and Oaxaca's Day of the Dead sell out months in advance.
  • Pack layers: The Dolomites can have snow even in early summer, while Oaxaca is warm year-round.
  • Learn basic phrases: A few words in Japanese, Italian, Spanish, or Maori go a long way with locals.
  • Consider travel insurance: Protect your bucket list adventures, especially for adventure activities.
